# Appendix H — Instruments

## H.1 Scope

This appendix addresses the requirement to include any survey instruments, questionnaires, or interview schedules used in the research. This declaration is explicitly included to satisfy the submission template.

## H.2 Declaration

This study is a quantitative systems-engineering and software-development project. It does not involve the collection of data from human participants through surveys, interviews, observation, focus groups, or any other instrument-based elicitation method. Accordingly, **not applicable**: no human participants were recruited, no instruments were designed, administered, or used, and no raw instrument data exist in this repository.

## H.3 Rationale

The research objective was to design, implement, and evaluate SCAFAD — a privacy-preserving, multi-layer anomaly detection framework for serverless computing. All evaluation data consist of synthetically generated AWS Lambda telemetry records. These records do not carry personal information about any individual and were generated programmatically by the evaluation harness. No ethical obligation to administer instruments arose.

The absence of instruments is consistent with the ethical clearance documented in Appendix A (ethics form) and Appendix B (ethics approval), both of which confirm that no human-participant involvement was required. The research follows a computer-science empirical-evaluation paradigm in which the artefact itself — the running SCAFAD system — is the subject of measurement, not human respondents.

Any future extension of this work that incorporates user-acceptance testing, practitioner interviews, or deployment surveys would require a new ethics application and the design of appropriate instruments. Such an extension is noted as future work in Chapter 11 but falls outside the scope of this submission.
