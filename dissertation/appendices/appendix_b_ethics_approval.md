# Appendix B — Ethics Approval / Exemption

## B.1 Overview

This appendix documents the ethics review outcome for the SCAFAD project. Because the project involves no human-participant data, no primary data collection from individuals, and no sensitive data, it qualifies for an **exemption** from full ethics committee review under Birmingham Newman University's tiered ethics framework.

## B.2 Basis for Exemption

The ethics screening form (Appendix A) confirms:

1. **No human participants** are involved in any phase of the project.
2. **No human-participant data** are collected, processed, stored, or analysed.
3. The evaluation corpus consists exclusively of synthetically generated AWS Lambda telemetry records produced by the project's own evaluation harness.
4. No sensitive data categories (health, financial, biometric, location, or political data) are used.
5. No deception, coercion, or power-imbalance situations arise.
6. The research poses no physical, psychological, or legal risk to any individual.

Under the Newman University research ethics policy, projects that satisfy all six criteria above are **approved** at the self-assessment level and do not require escalation to the full Research Ethics Committee (REC).

## B.3 Exemption Confirmation

**Status:** Ethics exemption granted at self-assessment level.

**Reviewer:** Academic supervisor (as the designated first-line ethics reviewer for undergraduate projects).

**Date:** April 2026.

**Outcome:** The project is **signed off** as ethically compliant. It may proceed to submission without further REC review.

## B.4 GDPR Compliance Note

Although no human-participant data are collected, the dissertation discusses GDPR compliance as an object of study — specifically, the obligation to apply privacy-preserving techniques to telemetry that *could* contain personally identifiable information in a production serverless environment. The project's Layer 1 conditioning pipeline (hashing and redaction) is designed to demonstrate GDPR-compatible data handling. This design discussion does not itself constitute the processing of personal data.
